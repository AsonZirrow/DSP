

# Research Interests

## Overview

My engineering interests focus on designing software systems that are modular, explainable, and maintainable.

Rather than concentrating on a single application domain, I am interested in architectural patterns that allow systems to evolve while remaining understandable and predictable.

---

# Current Areas of Exploration

## Modular Software Architecture

Designing systems composed of independent components with clearly defined responsibilities.

---

## Explainable Decision Systems

Building deterministic systems whose decisions can be inspected, reproduced, and understood.

---

## Information Representation

Developing structured representations that improve organization, extensibility, and reasoning.

---

## Engineering Tooling

Creating reusable tools that automate software analysis, evaluation, and organization.

---

## Software Quality

Applying testing, documentation, and modular design to improve maintainability and long-term evolution.

---

# Relationship to This Portfolio

Each repository explores one aspect of this broader research direction.

**Routing Decision Engine**

Explores how computation and workflows can be organized into configurable decision pipelines.

**Metadata Extraction Toolkit**

Explores how information can be extracted, structured, and analyzed through modular components.

**Rule-Based Decision Engine**

Explores how explicit rules can evaluate information to produce deterministic and explainable outcomes.

Together, these projects investigate different architectural approaches to building reliable software systems.

---

# Future Directions

Areas of future exploration include:

* Resource optimization
* Multi-stage decision systems
* Repository intelligence
* Static analysis
* Explainable automation
* AI engineering infrastructure

---

# Long-Term Goal

My long-term goal is to design engineering systems that combine modular architecture with transparent decision-making, enabling software that remains understandable, extensible, and reliable as complexity increases.
